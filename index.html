Climate Change

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in Earth's climate caused by human activities, primarily the release of greenhouse gases. It leads to global warming, rising sea levels, and various environmental and societal impacts. Global efforts, such as the Paris Agreement, aim to reduce emissions and limit temperature rise. Individuals can also contribute by conserving energy and supporting sustainable practices.

Trees Undo Climate Change

Trees help combat climate change by absorbing carbon dioxide (CO2) from the atmosphere, acting as natural carbon sinks. They also provide cooling effects and support biodiversity. However, they are just one part of the broader effort to address climate change, which includes reducing emissions and other strategies.

Global Warming

Global warming is the long-term increase in Earth's average surface temperature, mainly caused by human activities like burning fossil fuels, which release greenhouse gases. This leads to rising global temperatures, melting ice caps, more extreme weather events, rising sea levels, ocean acidification, and disruptions to ecosystems and human health. Mitigating global warming requires reducing greenhouse gas emissions and transitioning to sustainable energy sources, with international agreements like the Paris Agreement aimed at limiting temperature increases. Individual actions like conserving energy and using renewables can also make a difference in addressing this critical issue.
